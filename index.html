Roadmap to MIT – Master's or PhD in Computer Science (via IIT)

Phase 1: Class 12 (2025–2026)

Academics

  • Physics: 95+
  • Chemistry: 95+
  • Maths: 95+
  • English: 90+
  • Computer Science/IP: 95+ (if applicable)

Entrance Exams

  • JEE Main (Jan & April 2026): Target 99.5+ percentile
  • JEE Advanced (May 2026): Target Top 500 rank

Goal

Get into IIT Bombay/Delhi/Madras/others with CS or related branch.

Phase 2: BTech at IIT (2026–2030)

1. Academic Excellence

  • Target CGPA: 9.0+/10

2. Research

  • Work under IIT professors from Year 2
  • Apply to MITACS, DAAD, SN Bose internships
  • Publish 1–2 research papers in top conferences (NeurIPS, ICML, IEEE)

3. Coding & Projects

  • Contribute to Open Source
  • Build serious AI/ML or Systems Projects
  • Participate in Hackathons, GSoC, Competitions

4. Exams

  • GRE (mid-2029):
    • Verbal: 160+
    • Quant: 168–170
    • AWA: 4.5+
  • TOEFL/IELTS (late 2029):
    • TOEFL: 110+
    • IELTS: 7.5+

Phase 3: MIT Application (Aug–Dec 2029)

Documents

  • SOP: Strong motivation and research alignment
  • LORs: From IIT professors you've worked with
  • Resume: Highlight GPA, projects, papers, internships

Apply to:

  • PhD in EECS at MIT (May exit with SM – Master of Science – in 2–3 years)

Deadline:

Around December 15, 2029

MIT Program Structure
